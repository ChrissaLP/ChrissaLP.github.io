Corduroy Takes a Bow: A New Look at a Classic

The release of Corduroy Takes A Bow marks the 50th anniversary of the publication of the classic book Corduroy by Don Freeman. What was unusual about the original book was not its eponymous main character - a teddy bear named Corduroy - but its heroine, Lisa, an African-American child who rescues Corduroy from a department store.

The new Corduroy book brings the overly curious bear to a new setting: the theater. The book's bright illustrations convey the wonder of the theater to a young child. A child can also relate to the relationship between Lisa and her bear. The story has all the adventure and heart of the original and is worth adding to your child's bookshelf. - Reviewed November 20, 2018

Rating: ★ ★ ★ ★

Mossy: A Tale of an Extraordinary Turtle

This lushly illustrated book by Jan Brett transports the reader back to an earlier time. The detailed illustrations bring us to the world of an Edwardian natural history museum and its surrounding garden. The book also introduces the character of Mossy, a turtle with a garden growing on her shell, who becomes a temporary resident of the museum.

The story is simple in comparison to the exquisite illustrations, but it presents an effective parable for how humans can better respect their animal neighbors. An especially great book for budding naturalists! - Reviewed October 31, 2018

Rating: ★ ★ ★ ☆
